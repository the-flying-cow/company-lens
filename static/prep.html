2. The STAR Method

Structure your behavioral answers to provide clear, impactful narratives. Relate these to your own life and create a bank of 4 to 5 versatile stories before the interview.

S Situation: Set the scene and context.
T Task: Describe your specific responsibility.
A Action: Explain exactly what YOU did and how.
R Result: Share the measurable outcome.
Example: "During a product launch (Situation), I needed to reduce server load by 20% (Task). I implemented a new caching layer using Redis (Action), which resulted in a 35% decrease in load times and zero downtime during launch (Result)."

3. Resume Mastery

Revise your resume thoroughly. You must be able to speak deeply about everything written on it. If it is on the page, it is fair game. Prepare to explain the "why" and "how" behind every project, technology, and metric you have listed.
